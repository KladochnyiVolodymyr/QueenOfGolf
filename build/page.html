8 Amazing Facts About Kenya’s Eight Year Old Queen Of Golf Chanelle Wangari

Tuko.co.ke spent a morning with her at the Muthaiga Golf Club where she was working on her swing with the help of her coach Charles Farrar.

12 august 2015 5201

Exit Tiger Woods and Phil Mickelson. Enter Chanelle Wangari, an eight year old Kenyan golfer who wants to give Woods a run for his money.

Wangari is a class three student at Makini School. By the way, she turns nine in September but before then she wants to have won the the US Kids Golf World Championship that will be held in Pinehurst, North Carolina from July 30th to August 1, 2015.

Here are 8 amazing facts about Wangari…

Eight Wangari does all the things eight year old’s do
8

She goes to class during the week and has homework which she endeavors to finish by Friday afternoon so she can play golf.

“It is a lot of running, we have to make it to practice a bunch of times in a week. There are a lot of early mornings and cramped schedules, especially ahead of a big tournament, but Chanelle loves golf, which makes it all worth it,”

says Maureen Wangari, Chanelle’s mother.

Wangari says she enjoys school very much

“I love math, numbers are really fun. They help me with golf, you can see how you’re doing in the game, just by using math problems.”

Seven Long attention span – but for golf
7

When Wangari is on the golf course, the game is the only thing on her mind. Yes! No getting distracted. When she plays at the US Kids Golf World Championship, it will be her first time to travel to the US.

This is what Wangari said when I asked her what she wanted to know about North Carolina.

“How is the humidity? It will affect the distance I can hit the ball. I really can’t wait to go to Disney World, right after the competition we are going there and I am so excited!”

Six Travels because of golf
6

To qualify for the US Kids Golf World Championship, Wangari had to be top three in the Africa Junior Golf Championship that was held in South Africa in September 2014. She has played in Malaysia and will travel to Italy in October of 2015 to play.

“I’m very excited to go to Europe to golf. I haven’t been there before and I want to play on their courses, I hear they’re really beautiful,”

she says.
